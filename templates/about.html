Competition Format:

The format of the NCAA Fencing Championships is as follows: for each weapon-gender (men's and women's sabre, épée, and foil), there are 24 competitors. Some schools have entered as many as 12 competitors across the various events, while others have just one. No event can have more than two competitors from any one school.

Every competitor fences 23 five touch bouts over the course of their two day event (i.e. each competitor fences every other competitor). The three men's events are fenced on Thursday March 24th, and Friday March 25th, while the women's events are fenced on Saturday March 26th and Sunday March 27th. A more detailed schedule is given below.

The six individual championships are determined by a four person bracket made up of the top four individual finishers in each event. Fifteen touch bouts then determine the winner of the individual championship.

The team championship is determined by aggregating the number of wins in the regular competition (i.e. the fifteen-touch individual championship bouts have no weight on the outcome of the team championships). The team with the most wins is crowned team champion.

Website Format:

This website helps followers of the NCAA Fencing Championships observe how close a team is to winning this championship. For all schools, the school's place in the competition, and the amount of bouts it has won (with the school's win percentage in parentheses).

The school in the top row is the school in first place. For this school, the number of bouts a school needs to "clinch" the championship is displayed (with the win percentage needed to "clinch" in parentheses). A school has "clinched" (i.e. won the NCAA Championship), when no school, even with a 100% win percentage, could surpass said school's toal bouts. When the first place school has clinched, a special message will indicate this.

For all other schools, the number of bouts they need to win to catch the leading school is displayed (with the win percentage needed to take the lead in parentheses).
